Software Development

Even when I was just a young kid, I had this fascination for computers. There was something so seemlessness and impressive about them that drew me to them. Maybe it was the speed and efficiency of them, or maybe the fact that there were endless things to do on them. I have always loved computers and how they work, so when I found out that I could make it my career, I knew that I had found my calling in life.

My experience with coding started young. There was a summer camp called "iD Tech," which was held at Colorado State University many years ago. There I learned how to make a very simple mod for a video game, but ever since then, I was hooked to the process, and my love for computers only grew stronger.

Into highschool and college, I began to dive deeper into the field, and began to learn all the fundamentals of coding. It didn't matter what flavor of Computer Science there was, I was interested in it all. However, I grew particularly fond of Systems Engineering and Software Development. The concept of being able to see a problem and do something to solve it is an awesome thing, and I always find fulfillment out of being able to solve problems for other people.

Bronson is a web development project I've been working on for the University of Wyoming, and has been one of the most exciting projects I have had the privilege to work on while at UW. When I first joined the IT department with the Classroom Technology Services (CTS) division, I just started out as a student field technician - going around and fixing the technology in all the classrooms across campus. A few years before I joined the team, a couple of the techs had started Bronson as a CTS project, a tool designed to condense all of the tools and services that a student tech needs into one place - a website that can be accessed anywhere on campus. Talking with those techs, I became more interested in their goal, and I wanted to contribute. As a field tech I saw a way to add to it with my own tool, Tickex - a tool where techs can look at tickets and respond to support requests.

The University of Wyoming uses TeamDynamix's ticketing system to track maintenence requests, issues, tutorials, etc... Our division, CTS, heavily relies on this system, so I saw it fit that Bronson have capabilities to serve tickets to field techs. Tickex uses many common web application techniques which includes API handling, user authentication, server side/client side development, and intelligent organization algorithms to function as efficiently as possible - all autonomously! It has been a large undertaking, but I am proud to say that Tickex is now fully integrated into Bronson, and is planned for deployment to all field techs in the near future.

In order to graduate from the University of Wyoming's Computer Science Department, undergraduates develop a senior capstone project that encapsulates all the skills required of the degree. For my senior capstone project, a group of 4 of my peers and I are making a video game called "Primetime Paradox" using the Unity game engine and C# programming language. It is a rougue-like rhythm game where the player is dungeon crawling to the beat of the music, fighting enemies, and collecting items along the way. This project has been a great way to learn more about game development, and how to work in a team environment on a complex project.

We are making every aspect of the game, from the 3D models, to the music and sound effects, and even the back-end programming and game mechanics that run the game. In this project, my job is to act as the sound engineer and back-end systems developer. I am to make sure the game runs smoothly and efficiently. Since Primetime Paradox is a rhthm game, I had to make the beat detection system from scratch, and make it portable for all aspects of the game (game mechanics, animation, & event triggering). Unity as a game engine has been a great way to learn more about game development and systems development, and I am excited to see where this project takes us!


Nowadays, I am actively working on web development for the University of Wyoming IT department, working on my senior capstone project, developing various personal projects in my spare time, and keeping my technical skills sharp. I am always looking for new challenges and opportunities to grow as a developer and a person. I always seek to contribute to innovative projects, and am excited for what the future holds!

Music & Audio Technology

Ever since I was a child, I have been involved in music, taking music lessons as young as 5 year old! Piano and percussion were my main focus growing up. And as I got older, music theory became of interest. In highschool I've done many different types of percussion performance, from your standard concerpt percussion, to marching band, and even drumset drumming for a theatrical musical play.

Most noteable of my live musician career would be my participation with Drum Corps International (DCI) and Winter Gaurd Internation (WGI) where I was able to play at the highest level of competative percussion performance. In DCI, I marched with the Battalion Drum and Bugle Corps (2021 & 2022 seasons) and the Blue Knights Drum and Bugle Corps (2023 & 2024 seasons). For both seasons with Battalion, we competed well enough to make Open Class Finals. In 2023 I was fortunate enough to compete well enough with the Blue Knights to make it into World Class Finals - the top 12 corps in the world. In WGI, I was with the Blue Knight Inddor Percussion Ensemble for the longest (2022-2025 seasons). In 2022, I was fortunate enough to compete well enough with the Blue Knights Percussion to make it into World Class Finals - the top 15 percussion ensembles in the world.


On top of being a live musician and percussionist, I have a considerable amount of music experience in composition with electronic music production, DJing, and live audio technology experience managing and mixing entire stage productions. I have recieved my Audio Technology certificate from the University of Wyoming with an emphasis in music production and stage logistics.

While at the University of Wyoming, I had the opportunity to work for the Union's Student Technical Services (STS) department, and UWIT's Classroom Technology Services (CTS). STS was an AV technician position, where it was my job to setup, sound check, operate, and tear down entire stage sets with live musicians. My duties at CTS were to go around campus, installing and monitoring room audio and video systems. With both jobs, I had many performances and hundreds of rooms that I have worked where I have run into technical issues I have been able to resolve quickly.
