Software Development

My journey with Software Development has been one of the most influencial parts of who I am. I have always loved computers and how they work, so when I found out that I could make my field of study, I knew that I had found what I wanted to do in life.

My first experience with coding was when I was still a kid with the "ID Tech" Summer Camp, held at Colorado State University. There I learned how to make a very simple mod for a video game, but ever since then, I was hooked to the concept, and my love for computers grew stronger.

Into highschool and college, I began to dive deeper into the field, and began to learn all the fundamentals of coding. It didn't matter what flavor of Computer Science there was, I was interested in it all. However, I grew particularly fond of Systems Engineering and Software Development. The concept of being able to see a problem and do something to solve it is an awesome thing, and I always find fulfillment out of being able to solve problems for other people.

Bronson, the web development project I've been working on for the University of Wyoming, has been one of the most exciting projects I have had the privilege to work on. When I first joined the IT department with the Classroom Technology Services (CTS) division, I just started out as a student field technician - going around campus fixing the technology in all the classrooms. A few of the techs had started Bronson as a CTS project, and as a field tech I saw a way to add to it with my tool, Tickex.

The University of Wyoming uses TeamDynamix's ticket system to track maintenence requests, issues, tutorials, etc... Our division, CTS, heavily relies on this system, so I saw it fit that Bronson have capabilities to serve tickets to field techs. My Tickex application includes API handling, user authentication, front-end and back-end development, and intelligent organization algorithms to function as efficiently as possible - all autonomously!
